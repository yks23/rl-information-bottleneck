Bounded information

Each prompt has a small window where the verifier is alive.

The action-entropy shrinkage view is deliberately simple: rollouts begin as a broad distribution and training sharpens them. Once a prompt is solved or hopeless under the current setup, its binary verifier contributes little new information.

That is why adding prompts matters, but not perfectly. Dataset information is near-additive when prompts barely interact; it is sub-additive when transfer directions collide or duplicate each other.

The fun part

Why does math RL still improve today?

Because frontier labs are collectively playing the role of the missing AlphaZero opponent. Researchers, annotators, eval builders, data miners, contest problems, synthetic generators, and new model releases keep pushing a harder frontier back at the model.

It works, but it is a very expensive imitation of self-play. As models get stronger, humans alone cannot supply enough fresh, well-verified, sharply targeted failures. The endpoint is AI systems that generate, verify, and organize their own next frontier.
